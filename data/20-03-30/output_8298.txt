SAM OWENS/ COURIER &amp; PRESS  Interested in this topic ? You may also want to view these photo galleries :  EVANSVILLE , Ind .
-- With more than 3,500 vulnerable citizens to help in six counties , the Southwestern Indiana Regional Council on Aging is working with the Vanderburgh County Prosecutor 's Office to deliver essential household goods to those who need them .
Because of the coronavirus pandemic , thousands of vulnerable citizens who are at a higher risk of transmitting the virus in Vanderburgh County have been instructed to shelter in place .
SWIRCA employees have been fielding hundreds of phone calls every day @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ due to the quarantine .
To help those residents who may have trouble obtaining goods on their own because of their age or disability , the Prosecutor 's Office and SWIRCA have created a Vulnerable Citizen 's Relief Fund .
In anticipation that the quarantine conditions may be extended , the two agencies are working together to collect the necessary food and personal hygiene items and deliver them .
The community is being asked to donate the following supplies and food items :  toilet paper  paper towels  bleach  wipes ( disinfecting and personal )  shampoo/conditioner  deodorant  soap  laundry detergent  canned goods  canned meat  protein bars  pasta  instant potatoes  peanut butter  microwavable meals  shelf-stable snack foods  Monetary donations to the relief fund also will be accepted .
Beginning Wednesday , April 1 , donations can be delivered directly to SWIRCA , at 16 W .
Virginia St .
in Evansville , 8 a .
-2 p .
every Monday through Friday .

Prosecutor 's Office and SWIRCA employees will organize the donations and create drop-off baskets for vulnerable citizens .
To decrease the risk of transmission , the teams will then safely deliver the completed baskets and/or facilitate pick-up to SWIRCA 's growing list of clients .
For questions on how to help or to apply for help , call the Vulnerable Citizen Relief Hotline at 812-464-7800 .

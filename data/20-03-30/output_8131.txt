SEBASTIAN -- Sebastian River Medical Center announced Friday the hospital is now implementing ' telehealth doctor visits to limit person-to-person contact amid the rising spread of the novel coronavirus .

"  The telehealth video calls can be conducted through a smartphone , desktop or laptop that has access to a web camera , according to the company 's announcement .
The calls should " give patients peace of mind that they can receive the medical care they need without having to go to the doctor 's office , " according to Steward Medical Group .
The calls can be used for patients who may exhibit flu-like symptoms and want to speak with health professional without traveling to a medical center out of fear of contracting or spreading COVID-19 , according to the release .
Telehealth calls are available for primary care and internal medicine , according to the announcement .
Specialized care such as cardiology , orthopedics and women 's health also are available through the video calls .
" Telehealth visits will provide our patients the care they need and want , as well as mitigate @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ doctors from the comfort and security of their own home , " wrote Michael Callum , president of Steward Medical Group .
The company , which practices in 11 states and has over 3,000 medical providers , said patients should contact their doctor 's office to schedule the telehealth appointments .
The medical center will send an email invitation with the appointment time and instructions on how to download the conferencing software .
Options for in-person doctor 's visit are still available at the hospital , the announcement said .
Sebastian River has over 100 physicians , 650 nursing staffers and about 200 volunteers .
For more information , the public can call the hospital at 772-589-3186 .

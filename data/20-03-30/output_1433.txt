California Speeds Up Humanitarian Works Amid COVID-19 Pandemic  Announces $100 Million Fund To Tackle Homelessness  N24 Correspondent , SACRAMENTO : The California Military Department service members of the state government of California have come on duty from all around the state to perform vital humanitarian support missions to local and state agencies in response to the Coronavirus or the COVID-19 pandemic .
According to a statement released by the Department , this move follows an alert from California Governor Gavin Newsom last week to work together to minimize the spread of Coronavirus in the state .
Airmen from the Fresno-based , 144th Fighter Wing , California Air National Guard , deployed their Medical Detachment 1 's Homeland Response Force to support the California Emergency Medical Services Authority .
Working alongside their counter-parts , the Airmen will assist at a medical supply warehouse in Sacramento .
Likewise , 10 Airmen from the 144th Fighter Wing were deployed to Pacific Grove , California , where they are carrying out humanitarian works with the care of 19 quarantined passengers from the Grand Princess Cruise ship .

According to a statement from the Business , Consumer Services and Housing Agency of California , Governor Gavin Newsom signed SB 89 on March 17 , 2020 , which was approved unanimously by the state Legislature .
SB 89 authorized a total appropriation of up to $1 billion to provide assistance to help fight the spread of COVID-19 .
On March 18 , 2020 , Governor Newsom 's Administration allocated $100 million in emergency funding from SB 89 to local governments to help protect this vulnerable population and reduce the spread of COVID-19 by safely getting individuals into shelter and providing immediate housing options .
The Business , Consumer Services and Housing Agency 's Homeless Coordinating and Financing Council sent out award letters on Monday , March 24 , 2020 .

Singapore eases monetary policy  Singapore central bank eased its monetary policy by reducing the slope of its policy band as the state is expected to enter a deep recession due to the interruptions to economic activity caused by the outbreak of coronavirus .
Singapore The bank will adopt a zero percent per annum rate  The Monetary Authority of Singapore , said on Monday , that the bank will adopt a zero percent per annum rate of appreciation of the policy band starting at the prevailing level of the S$NEER .
There will be no change to the width of the policy band .
" This policy decision hence affirms the present level of the S$NEER , as well as the width and zero percent appreciation slope of the policy band going forward , thus providing stability to the trade-weighted exchange rate , " the bank said .
The MAS applies the exchange rate against a basket of currencies within an undisclosed band as its monetary policy tool .
The bank said the stable monetary policy stance reflects the primary role of fiscal policy in mitigating the economic impact of covid-19 .

MADISON , Wis .
-- Gov .
Tony Evers and state health leaders say Wisconsin is preparing to open field hospitals in anticipation of a surge in COVID-19 patients , as the number of cases is expected to peak in a week or more .
Evers said Monday that , " We are headed into the worst of this .
"  There have been more than 1,200 confirmed cases of coronavirus infections in the state as of Monday , including 20 deaths .
That 's based on state and local health department numbers .
Andrea Palm , secretary of the state Department of Health Services , says the effects of the stay-at-home order wo n't be seen until next week .

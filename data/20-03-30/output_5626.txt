TREND:Low rollovers , bearish FII bets indicate more pain for Nifty 50  Friday , Mar 27  By Apoorva Choubey  MUMBAI -- Tepid rollovers to the April series and high number of bearish bets by foreign portfolio investors suggest that the underlying worry over the human and economic fallout from the coronavirus pandemic still prevails .
Though the Nifty 50 has made a swift recovery in the past three sessions on the back of measures by central banks and governments the world over , most investors remain apprehensive of the current levels sustaining , as coronavirus cases show no sign of peaking , both globally and in India .
The net short positions of foreign portfolio investors in index futures outnumbered the long ones by 49,000 contracts as on Thursday , brokerage firm Sharekhan said , advising clients to stick to a " sell-on-rise " strategy .
What is also worrisome , analysts said , is low open interest in the April futures of the Nifty 50 , as it implies that traders have chosen not to carry forward positions due to caution .

The high levels of volatility gauge , India VIX , also show that there is quite a lot of bearishness in the market and wild swings can not be ruled out , said Chandan Taparia , head of derivatives at Motilal Oswal Securities .
Rollovers to the Nifty 50 's April futures were at 62% compared with the three-month average of 69% , while those in the Nifty Bank were at 55% versus the average of 70% , Dolat Capital Market said in a report .
Whatever positions did get rolled over were short ones , especially in the Nifty 50 , Nifty Bank and some large banking stocks .
The worry over rise in unsecured loan defaults due to job losses stemming from the coronavirus outbreak could continue to keep banking stocks under pressure .
Brokerage Sharekhan expects weakness in the market to continue in April , and sees the Nifty 50 falling to 6500-7000 points .

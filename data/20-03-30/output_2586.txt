PDP senators , CSOs demand judicious use of COVID-19 funds  Members of the opposition Peoples Democratic Party in the Senate have demanded proper monitoring and utilisation of funds and palliatives , meant to assist Nigerians as the nation intensify its fight against coronavirus .
The PDP caucus in the red chamber insisted that the disbursement of the palliative measures must be done in a way that would ensure that it gets to the people it is meant for .
The senators also urged relevant security agencies to be humane in their approach while enforcing the implementation of the measures that has to do with the restrictions of movement of Nigerians .
These were contained in a statement issued on Monday by the Minority Leader of the Senate , Senator Enyinnaya Abaribe .
Although , the PDP lawmakers commended the President , Major General Muhammadu Buhari ( retd .
) for his Sunday night broadcast , they however , noted that the National Assembly should have been consulted before making some of the statutory policy pronouncements .

" These are perilous times in the world and indeed an unusual situation for our dear country .
" So , the PDP caucus describes as a step in the right direction the President 's broadcast and policy measures , which we believe while being short on prioritising the poor and vulnerable in our society , nevertheless if properly implemented will keep our people safe from this deadly virus .

It added that doing so , would certainly put the ravaging virus at bay .
" It could be tough , but the measures are necessary now if we must survive this deadly scourge and emerge stronger after , " it added .
Also , the Civil Society Legislative Advocacy Centre and Transparency International in Nigeria on Monday called for total accountability of the use and distribution of the funds donated by groups and individuals towards combating coronavirus in the country .

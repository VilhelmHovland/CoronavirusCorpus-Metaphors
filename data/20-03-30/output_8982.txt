The decision to push back the Games by a full year from the original date of July 24 provides a long window of opportunity for the world to overcome the virus , Tokyo 2020 Organizing Committee President Yoshiro Mori said in announcing the decision .
Mori said the one-year delay would also give athletes , spectators and event organizers enough time to prepare for the rescheduled event .
After weeks of confusion about the fate of the Olympics as global sports shut down , Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe and International Olympic Committee President Thomas Bach agreed last week to delay the Games by up to a year .
The IOC said the decision would help protect the health of athletes and limit disruption to the international sporting calendar .

Now that the new timing of the Games has been confirmed , the organizers said they would work on securing all the necessary venues and work out additional costs , as well as who will pay for them .
Under one estimate from an academic in Japan , extra expenses could reach close to $6 billion .
Local corporate sponsors have stumped up more than $3 billion for the Games , but their contracts end this summer .
Toshiro Muto , local organizing committee chief executive officer , said the committee would talk to sponsors about extending their involvement with the Olympics .

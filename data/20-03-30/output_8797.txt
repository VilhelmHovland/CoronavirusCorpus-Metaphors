On Monday , the Mississippi Center for Justice launched a Mississippi mask drive asking volunteers to sew cloth masks in order to protect Mississippians vulnerable to COVID-19 .
Hundreds of finished protective masks wait to be bagged up as Blue Delta Jean Company in Verona , Mississippi on Thursday , March 26 , 2020 , have shifted their production from high-end blue jeans to producing 50,000 masks per week .
The companies first @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ ( Photo : Thomas Wells , AP )  The masks will be for workers and residents living in facilities such as prisons , jails , mental health institutions and restitution centers .
" Every person who can sew or learn how to sew a mask can make a tremendous difference right now , " saidPaloma Wu , Deputy Director of Impact Litigation at MCJ .
" MCJ has joined other groups in urging for release of residents who do not pose public safety concerns so that facilities can better contain an outbreak .
When all responsible population reduction measures have been exhausted , staff and individuals who remain need masks , " Wu said .
The Mississippi Mask Drive is partnering with the Cooperative Baptist Fellowship of Mississippi , Catholic Charities and the Catholic Diocese of Jackson .

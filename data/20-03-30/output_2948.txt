**25;3604;TOOLONG  Nova Scotia 's top doctor says community spread of COVID-19 has still not been confirmed .
Robert Strang , the province 's chief medical officer , says most of the new cases announced Sunday were connected to known cases or travel , but Public Health is looking into some cases that do n't yet have a clear connection .
" Some of our cases , there is concern that it might be community spread given what we know about it , but we can not make that firm conclusion until we 've finished our investigation and seen for sure whether they meet one of those criteria - we just ca n't explain @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ of separation away from a known case or travel , " explained Dr .
Strang .
He says no cases have been connected to gatherings Public Health has identified , including a St .
Patrick 's day party in Lake Echo .
Strang is urging Nova Scotians to continue with physical distancing and social isolation , as he says it is helping slow the spread of the novel coronavirus .
As of Monday , there are 122 cases of COVID-19 in Nova Scotia .
Seven people have fully recovered .

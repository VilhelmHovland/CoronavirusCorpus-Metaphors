HOLT -- When Lisa Weise 's sister-in-law told her that fighting coronavirus was draining her Detroit-area hospital 's supply of personal protective equipment , she wanted to help .
Weise , a biology and engineering teacher at Holt High School , worked with Superintendent David Hornak , Holt Junior High Science Technology Engineering Arts and Mathematics teacher Mary Wever and several other teachers to use the district 's 3D printers to help address @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ 11 of the school district 's 3D printers to produce face shields for healthcare workers .
They started planning the work Friday and , by Monday , had made at least 100 shields that will be provided to Sparrow Hospital or other local medical care providers , Wever said .
" You want to help , " Weise said .
" You see these folks over in the hospitals trying to do their job , but they need to be protected .
"  It takes from 45 to 90 minutes to make the headbands for the face shields .
Once the headbands are made , a laminate shield and elastic are attached .
Elastic , laminate , filament to make the headbands and monetary donations are being sought so the district can continue this work .
" We 'll keep doing it as long as there is a need , " Wever said .
Education institutions across the Lansing area have stepped up to help make and provide medical providers with the tools they need .
Michigan State University officials from the Colleges of Osteopathic @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ Resources , and Arts and Letters are using 3D printers to make face shields , according to a news release .
It 's all about doing what they can to help the doctors , nurses and workers trying to keep people safe , Wever said .
" The quick turnaround and the unwavering support of the medical field is huge in the community , " she said .

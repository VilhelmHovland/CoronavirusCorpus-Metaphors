Nunn : Providing support and resources for our Iowa businesses  The past weeks are a testament to the strength and resiliency of our communities .
In the weeks ahead , as we go on the offensive against the COVID-19 pandemic , we must ensure we provide the best possible healthcare for all Iowans and safeguard our economic drivers -- Iowa 's small and medium businesses .

To support our local communities , the state Legislature approved $525,000 to the State Hygienic Lab to ensure COVID-19 testing continues fully-funded over the next six-weeks .
Additionally , a $91 .
million appropriation was approved to cover the costs for Medicaid , Hawk-I , and the Glenwood Resource Center .
Together with our individual responsibilities to minimize social interaction and reduce community spread , we are all helping to prioritize the health and safety of our most vulnerable fellow Iowans .
Supporting main street  The economic impact of COVID-19 is crushing our main street businesses and hometown entrepreneurs .
Each day I speak directly with local business owners .
Barbers and hair stylists are paying rent on empty chairs , pet shop owners are taking care of stranded animals , and local manufactures are paying loans on idle equipment .

Relief plans for single business and sole proprietorship  Working with the team at IEDA and our local businesses , we 're setting up a direct process to get relief to single business owners in need .
Through the Targeted Small Business Sole Operator Relief Fund , jointly administered by IEDA and the Iowa Center for Economic Success , we will immediately begin providing grants of between $5,000 to $10,000 to sole proprietorship or single member LLC businesses that are also certified as Targeted Small Businesses by the state .
Businesses not currently certified TSBs may submit a certification application and be considered for assistance through the program .

Small and medium business assistance  With Gov .
Reynolds ' State Public Health Emergency Declaration , many of Iowa 's small and medium business were forced to close to help prevent the spread of COVID-19 .
The declaration also ensures emergency assistance for main street businesses by supporting our communities and employees during the outbreak .
More is needed and more will be provided , but this is a critical first-step during an unprecedented health and economic emergency .

Federal disasters assistance allows pandemic-impacted small and medium businesses to apply for low-interest support loans through the Small Business Administration .
The SBA is now making loans available statewide to small businesses and private , non-profit organizations to help alleviate economic injury caused by the coronavirus .
The Economic Injury Disaster Loans from the SBA offer up to $2 million in assistance and can provide vital economic support to small businesses to help overcome the temporary loss of revenue they are experiencing .
These loans may be used to pay fixed debts , payroll , accounts payable and other bills that can not be paid because of the disaster 's impact .

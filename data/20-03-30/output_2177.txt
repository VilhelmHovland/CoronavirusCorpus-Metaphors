The London region has recorded a half-dozen new cases of COVID-19 , including four people who recently travelled , health officials announced Sunday .
The latest cases bring the tally of people infected with the virus in London and nearby Middlesex County to 31 , including a man in his 70s who recently travelled to Portugal and died in hospital Friday .
One of the people infected lives in a long-term care home , while the other is a close contact of someone who recently travelled , the Middlesex-London Health Unit said .
Health officials are in close communication with the six infected persons and are following up with their close contacts , the health unit said .
The latest cases prompted the London region 's top public health official to renew his call for physical distancing in a bid to flatten the curve , a term used to prevent a spike in coronavirus cases that could overburden the health-care system .

On Saturday , Premier Doug Ford introduced steep fines for price gouging and reduced the maximum size of gatherings -- down to five from 50 -- as the province works to curb the economic impact of COVID-19 while stemming its spread .
Exceptions will be made for households with more than five family members .
Up to 10 people will be allowed to gather at funerals , and child-care centres looking after the kids of front-line workers can have up to 50 people on site .

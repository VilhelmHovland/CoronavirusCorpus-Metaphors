Citing coronavirus risk , ACLU sues for release of two ICE detainees at Essex County jail  Two federal immigration detainees have asked a court to free them immediately from Essex County 's jail , just days after a federal judge ordered the release of 10 other men in New Jersey to protect them from the coronavirus ,  The complaint was filed in U .
District Court in Newark on Sunday by the American @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ detainees , Mario Salazar and Mikhael Vasserman .
Both have chronic health conditions and are at risk because the jail is n't adequately protecting them from the virus , the lawsuit said .
" The dire situation in civil detention facilities makes the release of our clients a matter of life and death , " ACLU-NJ Senior Staff Attorney Farrin Anello said in a statement on Monday .
In detention , " close contact and a lack of access to basic supplies are facts of life -- it is simply impossible to maintain social distancing in these environments , which have become hotbeds for transmission .
"  The suit is the latest filed against U .
Immigration and Customs Enforcement by detainees across the country worried about the coronavirus , including complaints in Massachusetts , Washington and Arizona .
As of Monday , more than 120,000 cases had been reported in the U .
, with more than 2,100 deaths , according to figures from the CDC .
The Essex lawsuit names as defendants John Tsoukaris , an ICE field office director in Newark ; Matthew Albence , the @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ secretary of the Department of Homeland Security ; William Barr , the U .
attorney general ; and William Anderson and Guy Cirillo , the wardens of the Essex County jail .
Emilio Dabul , an ICE spokesman in Newark , and Anthony Puglisi , the county spokesman , said they could not discuss ongoing litigation .
Requests for comment from Homeland Security and the Attorney General 's Office went unanswered on Monday .
Essex County has an agreement with the federal government to house immigrants picked up by ICE and facing civil immigration violations .
As of Monday , four employees of the jail had tested positive for the novel coronavirus , as well as one immigration detainee held at the Newark facility , according to county figures .
Five other inmates tested positive at Delaney Hall , a privately run jail also in Newark .
Vasserman , according to the complaint , is a father who came to the United States over two decades ago as a refugee from Russia .
He had open-heart surgery before entering detention , as well as diabetes , high @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ , which said he has been held by ICE for 16 months .
Salazar , a father with young children , has lived in New Jersey for over a decade , has diabetes and high cholesterol , and has been coughing up blood , according to the suit .
Both men , the complaint states , sleep in dormitories at the jail that they share with several other men , some of whom they claim have had flu-like symptoms .
The men said they have limited access to soap and other hygiene products .
Given the close quarters at the Essex County Correctional Facility , it 's impossible to engage in effective social distancing , they allege .
" Clustering vulnerable individuals under these circumstances and waiting for COVID-19 to explode at the ECCF creates not only a humanitarian crisis , but also a constitutional one , ' ' the complaint states .
" Courts have long recognized that the Constitution forbids the government from allowing the people in its custody to suffer and die from infectious disease .
"  Weeks ago , jail officials @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ @ had started to screen new arrivals for symptoms .
Detainees who are 45 and older and have underlying health issues are monitored closely , and the jail has ordered extra gloves , soap , masks and other supplies , according to the county .
The move propelled immigrant advocates in New Jersey to step up their efforts demanding the release of detainees held at four facilities in the state .
On Friday , several protested outside the Bergen County Jail in Hackensack by driving their cars around the facility and honking their horns , and advocates gathered outside the Essex County jail earlier in the month .
